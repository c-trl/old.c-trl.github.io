This study aims to find symmetry in economic trends and news headline sentiment. It is expected that as economic downturn approaches, headline sentiment should begin to decrease. For example, warnings of impending recession before the Great Recession in 2007 in headlines should be reflected in significant decreases in news headline sentiment as late as Q3 2007. How accurate are news headlines in reflecting actual economic trends? This project looks to answer that question.

A timeline of economic trends will be defined which will be used to reference news headline sentiment against. Potential applications of this study include utilizing news headline sentiment as a possible predictor for economic trends. While prediction of the economy as a whole is a large attempt, perhaps certain individual economic indicators may be predictable by headline sentiment.

In this project:

  • Web data mining
  • HTML parsing
  • Natural Language Processing
  • Database creation/manipulation
  • Regression analysis

  • Real GDP

*Note that while these three variables will not necessarily be inter-correlated, we can use this plot as a reference to identify various events in history. We can see clearly where the Federal Reserve took action in anticipation/response to the Great Recession where the Federal Funds Rate begins to decline and drop the closest to 0 it has ever been. We can also see the delayed effects the recession had on the labor force - and more interestingly, the significant lag in recovery.

As far as Personal Savings Rate goes, historically (going back to 1959), the average rate is approximately 8.4%, and about 5% between 1995 and 2015. The unusually large spike towards 2013 is in fact the rate at 2012-12-01 which was 10.5% - the highest rate in 20 years. The graphed data displays an unusual (collective, almost) tendency to save during the holiday season. While counter-intuitive, this spike is followed by a period of lower average savings rate compared to that of the period between the beginning of the Great Recession and the 10.5% peak Personal Savings Rate.
